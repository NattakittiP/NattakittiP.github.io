About

I am a Thai student and early-career researcher currently studying in the International Bachelor Program in Informatics (IBPI) at Yuan Ze University, Taiwan. My academic path uniquely combines medical science and artificial intelligence, giving me an interdisciplinary foundation that connects human health, data, and computational reasoning.

I began my journey in Artificial Intelligence Engineering at Satit Prasarnmit Demonstration School, where I developed a Thai-language chatbot using natural language processing (NLP) and earned national recognition in robotics, placing 5th nationwide in the World Robot Olympiad (WRO).

After studying medicine for two years at Rangsit University, I realized that my real passion lies in understanding systems through data, logic, and algorithms rather than clinical practice alone. Transitioning to Yuan Ze University allowed me to integrate medical knowledge with informatics and machine learning — a combination that now drives my research and long-term goals.

My research interests include bioinformatics, machine learning validation, data-driven health systems, and AI for public policy innovation. I am particularly interested in building transparent and ethical ML pipelines that avoid data leakage, report realistic performance, and support evidence-based decision making in health and policy.

Research - First Author

Whole Blood Viscosity and Triglyceride Clearance: An Association Analysis Relevant to Cancer Risk (2025)

Accepted and selected for publication and oral presentation at IEEE ICBCB 2026 (EI Compendex & Scopus Proceedings)
Supervised by Dr. Qazi Mazhar ul Haq (Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Yuan Ze University)

This study proposes a data-driven risk stratification model using whole blood viscosity (WBV) and triglyceride clearance rate (TCR) as potential metabolic–oncologic markers. Using a retrospective cohort of 1,500 patients, I built a leakage-controlled machine learning pipeline to explore whether simple, routinely collected physiological and biochemical measurements can meaningfully predict cancer risk.

The work focuses on robust feature preprocessing, careful partitioning of data to prevent information leakage, and realistic performance evaluation. Beyond the numerical results, the study emphasizes the importance of methodological transparency in biomedical ML, especially when models are intended to inform early screening or risk communication in real-world health systems.

Bioinformatics · Hemorheology · Cancer Risk Modeling · Metabolic Dysregulation

Leakage-Controlled Machine Learning Reveals a Reproducible Null Association Between Whole Blood Viscosity and Triglyceride Clearance Rate (2025)

Journal manuscript in preparation for submission to Current Bioinformatics (Q3)
Supervised by Dr. Qazi Mazhar ul Haq (Assistant Professor, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Yuan Ze University)

Building on the same clinical cohort, this work investigates the relationship between WBV and TCR under a strict, leakage-controlled validation framework. By systematically addressing common sources of bias — such as patient-level overlap between training and testing sets and inappropriate feature scaling — the study demonstrates a reproducible null association between WBV and TCR.

Rather than forcing a positive result, the paper argues for the scientific value of well-documented null findings in bioinformatics. It shows how rigorous evaluation, clear reporting of negative results, and openly described pipelines can prevent overclaiming in biomedical machine learning and support more reliable translation of models into clinical and public health contexts.

Machine Learning · Bioinformatics · Data Leakage Control · Reproducible Research

Proctify – AI-Based Human Detection & Exam Integrity System (2025)

I developed Proctify, an AI-driven exam integrity system that uses face detection, eye tracking, and behavior analysis to identify actions that may indicate cheating or suspicious conduct. The system detects head movements, gaze direction, face absence, and irregular behavior patterns, then logs each event for human review. It combines automated human detection with manual verification to ensure fairness and reliability.
